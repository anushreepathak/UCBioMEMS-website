Biography
  • Hyoung Jin Cho earned his doctoral degree in electrical engineering (specialty: MEMS and BioMEMS) from the University of Cincinnati in 2002. He received his B.S. and M.S. degrees in Materials Science from Seoul National University, South Korea in 1989 and 1991, respectively. He was with KETI (Korea Electronics Technology Institute) from 1993 to 1997 as a research engineer. He developed various electroceramic components including PZT filters and multlayered chip inductors there. In 1997, He joined  the Center for Microelectronic Sensors and MEMS (CMSM) in the University of Cincinnati. He is currently doing his post doctoral research in Microsystems and BioMEMS Lab.
  • His research interests include 
    Core MEMS  - magnetic actuators and sensors
    BioMEMS and Microfluidic devices - valve, dispenser and pump
    MOEMS (Micro-Opto-Electro-Mechanical-Systems) - mirrors and switches
    MEMS fundamentals- UV-LIGA, electroplating and packaging. 
  • He is and was involved in DARPA and NASA projects.
